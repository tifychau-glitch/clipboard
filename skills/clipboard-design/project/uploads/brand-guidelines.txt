02 — LOGO SYSTEM
The Logo
The Clipboard logo consists of two elements: the icon mark and the wordmark. They should almost always appear together, with the icon mark to the left of the wordmark.
Icon Mark
The icon is a clipboard outline with three color-coded border segments — Yellow (left), Teal (right), and Violet (bottom) — with a navy checkmark inside. The three colors are intentional and represent the multi-functional nature of the platform.
Yellow segment = Contracts, tasks, deliverables
Violet segment = Agent intelligence, automation
Teal segment = Active, running, success states
Wordmark
The wordmark is always set in lowercase: &quot;clipboard&quot; — never &quot;Clipboard&quot; or &quot;CLIPBOARD&quot;. Lowercase communicates approachability and modernity, which is core to our brand personality.
Logo Variations
Default (Light)
Use on white or light backgrounds. Icon mark in color, wordmark in Navy.
Dark Mode
Use on dark/navy backgrounds. Icon mark in color, wordmark in White.
Icon Only
Use for app icons, favicons, profile images where space is limited.
Logo Clear Space
Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the lowercase &apos;c&apos; in the wordmark. Never crowd the logo with other elements.

Color Rules

The three accents (Yellow, Violet, Teal) are supporting cast — never competing equals.
Never use all three accent colors at full weight in the same component or section.

Yellow on Navy and Violet on White are your two highest-contrast pairings — use them for primary CTAs.

04 — TYPOGRAPHY
Type System
Clipboard uses a two-font system: Bricolage Grotesque for all display and headline text, and DM Sans for all body copy and UI text. DM Mono is used exclusively for labels, data readouts, and status text.
Display — Bricolage Grotesque
Work less. Do more.
Weights used: 800 (Display), 700 (H2), 600 (H3, UI labels)
Use for: Page headlines, section titles, card headers, button text, navigation
Letter spacing: -0.02em to -0.035em (tighter = more modern and confident)
Google Fonts: Bricolage Grotesque
Body — DM Sans
Clipboard gives your business a team of AI agents that handle the back office — so you can focus on closing deals, not managing tasks.
Weights used: 300 (descriptive body), 400 (standard body), 500 (emphasized body)
Use for: Paragraph text, descriptions, supporting copy, UI prose
Line height: 1.65–1.7 (generous spacing improves readability)
Google Fonts: DM Sans
Labels — DM Mono

Weights used: 400, 500
Use for: Status labels, data readouts, timestamps, metadata, section labels
Always: Uppercase, wide letter-spacing (0.10–0.18em)
Never: Use for headlines or paragraph body copy

05 — DESIGN PRINCIPLES
How We Design
Clipboard&apos;s design language is modern, minimal, and business-first. Every decision should make the product feel easier to trust and faster to use.
1. Navy Dominates
Navy (#12192B) is the primary color of every major surface — hero sections, sidebars, footers. It establishes credibility and gives our three accent colors room to pop. When in doubt, go navy.
2. One Accent Per Section
Each section or component should lead with one accent color. Yellow for CTAs. Teal for live/active states. Violet for product and agent-related UI. Never stack all three at equal weight — it creates visual noise.
3. The Checkmark Is a Brand Moment
The checkmark inside our icon isn&apos;t just a logo detail — it&apos;s a brand promise. &quot;Done. Handled. Complete.&quot; Lean into checkmark language in UI microcopy: &quot;Contract sent&quot;, &quot;Follow-up complete&quot;, &quot;Task handled.&quot; It reinforces what the product does every time someone reads it.
4. Rounded but Not Soft
Use border-radius of 10–14px for cards and buttons. This range feels professional and modern without tipping into consumer/cute territory. Avoid 20px+ radii which read as childlike. Avoid 0px radii which read as aggressive.
5. Breathe
White space is not empty space — it&apos;s doing work. The contrast between dense Navy sections and light, open content panels is what gives the brand its polish. Resist the urge to fill every surface. Let the design breathe.
6. Type Hierarchy Is Non-Negotiable
Headlines should always feel much larger and heavier than body copy. The jump from H1 to body text should be dramatic. This creates clarity and makes pages easy to scan — which matters enormously for business users who are busy.
