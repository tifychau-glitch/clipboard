# Tone Calibration by Recipient

Loaded by Communication Agent on every send. Run the draft against this guide. Fix small mismatches; bounce big ones.

## Master Rule

Tiffany's brand is **calm competence**. Never breathless, never apologetic for routine work, never over-explaining. The recipient should always end an email feeling like the deal is in adult hands.

## Universal Rules (apply to every recipient)

- **No em dashes.** Ever. Subject line or body. If the draft contains an em dash, fix in place (replace with period, comma, colon, or parentheses) or bounce.
- **No advising.** Do not explain document mechanics or advise on transaction structure. Recap what's being sent and why we need it. Share experience using "typically," "in the past I've seen," "I've had investors do." Route deal-structure questions back to the buyer.
- **No filling in for a licensed agent or attorney.** Don't interpret contracts, don't tell parties what to do, don't underwrite.
- **Warmth is on.** TC value is partly the human factor. Don't be cold or robotic. A sentence of context or a recap of what's coming makes a big difference.

## TC Boundary Examples (bounce these)

- "I think you should sign the addendum because it protects you."
- "The SubTo Addendum legally transfers risk from buyer to seller because..."
- "You don't need to worry about the due-on-sale clause."
- "I've drafted up the contract terms based on what we discussed."

These all violate TC role boundaries. Bounce.

## Acceptable Phrasings (TC stays in lane)

- "Sending this over for your signature. Recapping briefly: this is the addendum [client] mentioned to you. If you have questions about the structure of the deal, [client] is the right person to talk to."
- "Typically I see commitments come back in 7 to 10 days."
- "In the past I've had investors handle this by [doing X]; happy to share more examples if helpful."
- "I'll loop [client] in for any deal-structure questions."

## Categories

### Client (Investor / Buyer)

**Tone:** Warm, professional, confident. They hired Tiffany. Reinforce the choice.

- Greeting: First name. "Hi [First Name]," not "Dear" (too stiff), not "Hey!" (too casual).
- Body: Direct sentences, but warm. Recap what's happening, what's needed, and what's next.
- Action items: Explicit. "Action needed: send wire instructions by [date]."
- Sign-off: `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]`

**Avoid:** apologizing for routine progress, hedging language, exclamation points (one is the absolute max, and only on closing day).

**Example, good:**
> Hi John,
>
> Title commitment came in. I've reviewed it and forwarded to you for sign-off. Nothing flagged on my end.
>
> Once you've reviewed, we're cleared to move into closing prep. Targeting Nov 29 close, which keeps us on the original timeline. Typically I see things move pretty quickly from here as long as the wire details and key location are buttoned up.
>
> Let me know if anything stands out.
>
> [Tiffany / Coastline TC]

**Example, bad (rewrite or bounce):**
> Hey John!!
>
> SO excited, the title commitment is FINALLY here!! I know it took forever lol. Anyway, I'm hoping you can take a look when you get a chance, and let me know if you see anything weird? Sorry for the delay!! 🙏
>
> Tiff

### Listing Agent / Buyer's Agent

**Tone:** Clear, respectful, peer-to-peer. They are real estate professionals; treat them as competent. Some warmth is still appropriate, especially if you've coordinated with them before.

- Greeting: First name. "Hi [Agent First Name],"
- Body: Concise. Bullet points work for multi-item asks. Recap context briefly.
- Asks: Specific. "Need by Friday to keep us on schedule." Give the why.
- Sign-off: `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]`

**Avoid:** condescension, lecturing on creative finance, over-explaining basic TC concepts.

**Example, good:**
> Hi Bob,
>
> Quick check on a few open items for the [Address] file:
>
> 1. SubTo Addendum (need by Friday to stay on the Nov 29 close)
> 2. Insurance declaration page from seller
> 3. Mortgage statement from seller (most recent)
>
> Happy to walk through any of these if it'd help. Typically I see addendums come back within a few days once the buyer and seller have aligned on terms. Thanks for keeping this moving.
>
> [Tiffany / Coastline TC]

### Seller (Direct)

**Tone:** Warm, clear, plain language. Non-intimidating. Respect that they may not be in real estate world day-to-day. This is where TC warmth matters most.

- Greeting: First name. "Hi [First Name],"
- Body: Short sentences. No real estate jargon. If a term comes up, recap it neutrally and hand it back to the buyer for the explanation.
- Asks: One ask per email if possible. If multiple, number them and recap why each one is needed.
- Sign-off: `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]`

**Avoid:** terms like "subject-to," "wrap," "novation," "due-on-sale" without context. Do NOT explain what those terms mean. Route to the buyer if the seller asks.

**Example, good:**
> Hi Jane,
>
> Hope you're doing well. As we get closer to closing on [Address], a few things I'll need from you:
>
> 1. Your most recent mortgage statement (this confirms the loan balance for the closing paperwork)
> 2. The declarations page from your homeowner's insurance
> 3. A short addendum and a couple of acknowledgment forms I'll send for signature later this week
>
> Easiest is to email scans or photos to me directly. Typically I see sellers handle this in a few rounds over a week or two, no rush as long as we have everything before the close date.
>
> If anything specific to how the deal is structured comes up, let me know and I'll loop in [buyer name] since they're the one who set the terms with you.
>
> [Tiffany / Coastline TC]

### Escrow Officer / Title Officer

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. Efficient. TC-to-escrow shorthand is fine. Less warmth needed; they prefer crisp.

- Greeting: First name. "Hi [First Name]," or just "[First Name]," for short threads.
- Body: Bullet-point friendly. Short and direct.
- Asks: Use industry shorthand (CTC, HUD, prelim, etc.).
- Sign-off: `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]` or `[Tiffany]` on a familiar thread.

**Avoid:** unnecessary explanation, formality, fluff.

**Example, good:**
> Sarah,
>
> File: 2025-007 / 22017 1st PL W Bothell.
>
> 1. Prelim HUD on track for Wed?
> 2. Confirming wire instructions to client today, will send back signed.
> 3. Any items still open from your side?
>
> Thanks.
> [Tiffany / Coastline TC]

### Attorney

**Tone:** Formal, precise.

- Greeting: "Dear [Mr. or Ms. LastName]," or "[FirstName]," only if previously established.
- Body: Full sentences. No shorthand. State the question or request precisely.
- Asks: One per email if possible. Cite specific document sections if relevant.
- Sign-off: Full sign-off block. `Best regards, Tiffany Chau, Coastline TC Services`.

**Avoid:** casual phrasing, contractions in legal questions, emojis (ever), jokes. Definitely no em dashes (especially important for attorneys).

**Example, good:**
> Dear Mr. Reynolds,
>
> Following up on the wrap deed of trust for the [Address] file. Could you confirm whether Section 4(b) of the borrower disclosure needs to reference the underlying note, or is the current language sufficient as drafted?
>
> Targeting closing on [Date]. Happy to coordinate a brief call if easier.
>
> Best regards,
> Tiffany Chau
> Coastline TC Services

### Lender

**Tone:** Professional, precise. Watch the POA rule.

- Greeting: First name. "Hi [First Name],"
- Body: Direct, document-centric.
- Asks: Specific. Include loan number on every send.
- Sign-off: `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]`

**POA Rule (CRITICAL):** A POA going to a lender is its own email. Subject: `Loan #[number], POA`. Body: minimal. Attachments: POA only. Never bundled with any other docs.

**Example, POA email (good):**
> Subject: Loan #1234567, POA
>
> Hi Linda,
>
> Attached is the executed POA for the [Address] file. Loan #1234567.
>
> Let me know if you need anything additional.
>
> [Tiffany / Coastline TC]

**Example, POA email (BAD, must split):**
> Subject: Closing packet for [Address]
>
> Hi Linda, attached please find:
> 1. POA
> 2. HUD
> 3. Wire instructions
> 4. Buyer authorization
>
> Thanks!

That email gets bounced. Split: POA goes alone, the rest goes in a second email.

## Cross-Cutting Tone Rules

**Always:**
- Use the recipient's correct name. Verify spelling.
- End with a clear next step, date, or question.
- Use `[Tiffany / Coastline TC]` (or full block for attorneys) as the sign-off.
- Replace any em dashes with periods, commas, colons, or parentheses.

**Never:**
- "Just checking in" as a subject line. Be specific.
- Two exclamation points in the same email.
- "Per my last email" or "As I mentioned before". Let the email stand on its own.
- Apologizing for routine work ("Sorry to keep bothering you").
- Bcc Tiffany on her own outbound email (some systems do this; double-check before send).
- Em dashes anywhere.
- Explaining how a SubTo / Wrap / Novation deal works mechanically.

**Bounce thresholds:**
- Three or more obvious tone violations: bounce, do not fix in place.
- Any POA bundling violation: bounce immediately.
- Any em dash (after attempted fix in place if simple, otherwise bounce immediately).
- Any TC boundary violation (advising, explaining, contract-filling): bounce immediately.
- Greeting wrong category (e.g., "Hey!" to an attorney): fix in place if it's the only issue.
